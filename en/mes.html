The Cloud Problem

Why your 3D printers don't belong in the cloud.

Modern 3D printers from many manufacturers send print data, camera feeds and machine telemetry through external cloud servers by default. Convenient for home users. A problem for companies.

Production data, CAD geometries and manufacturing parameters are core knowledge. They do not belong on servers in the US or China, least of all in industries with documentation and data protection requirements.

leanAM MES keeps all data inside your network. The machines run without external servers or manufacturer accounts, and production continues during an internet outage.

Workflow

From STL file to finished part — fully local

This is how a print job flows through leanAM MES, documented and auditable. Not a single step touches an external server.

STEP 01

Upload

STL/3MF is uploaded via web UI or API — stored locally, with the ERP order linked automatically.

STEP 02

Slicing

Pick a profile or let the system suggest one. Material and machine are validated, and the slice job runs locally.

STEP 03

Routing

The job is routed to the next available machine — even across multiple shop-floor sites.

STEP 04

Production

Printing starts automatically while telemetry is logged locally. Any deviation triggers an immediate alert.

STEP 05

Documentation

Print log, material batch and images are archived locally — ready for audit and shipment.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions about leanAM MES

Answers to the questions we hear most often, from licensing model to data sovereignty.

Does leanAM MES run without internet access?

Yes. leanAM MES is fully on-premise and requires no internet connection. All data stays inside your network — no forced cloud, no subscription, no external servers.

Which 3D printers are supported?

leanAM MES supports several ways of controlling machines directly on the local network, without an external cloud and without mandatory accounts. Print data stays entirely within your infrastructure. Whether and to what extent your production environment can be supported is something we can review in a conversation. Contact us

Does our production data stay in-house?

Yes. leanAM MES runs fully on-premise — all production and machine data remains inside your network. No external servers, no cloud routing, no data sharing. Software and support come from Germany.

How long does implementation take?

A basic installation without ERP integration is typically ready for use within 1–3 working days. For more involved setups, such as ERP/CAQ integration, large machine fleets or specific compliance requirements, we work out a realistic schedule together. We usually plan 2–6 weeks to full operation.

Can leanAM MES be connected to SAP or other ERP systems?

leanAM MES provides open interfaces (REST, OPC UA, MQTT) that enable integration with ERP, CAQ and PLM systems. Where technically feasible, custom connectors can be developed for your existing system. We are happy to discuss which integration makes sense for your environment. Contact us

Is there a demo of leanAM MES?

Yes. We offer a 30-minute live demo built around your specific use case — free of charge and without obligation. Request a demo appointment via the contact form.
